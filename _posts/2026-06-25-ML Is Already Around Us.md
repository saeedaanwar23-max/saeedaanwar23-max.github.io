## ML Is Already Around Us

### I Thought ML Was Still in the Future

For a long time I thought Machine Learning was something that existed in research labs and tech giants — not in ordinary daily life. It felt distant. Something that would matter eventually but not yet. Dr. Bilal Ahmad shattered that idea completely in one single lecture.

### He Asked Us to Look at Our Phones

At UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, one afternoon Dr. Bilal Ahmad walked into class and asked everyone to take out their phones. Then he said — *"Everything on that screen that feels personal to you is Machine Learning at work."*

He went through examples one by one.

> *"When YouTube suggests a video you actually want to watch — that is ML. When your keyboard predicts your next word — that is ML. When your phone unlocks by recognizing your face — that is ML. It is not the future. It is already the present."*

### Healthcare, Agriculture, and Beyond

He did not stop at smartphones. He talked about how ML models are being used to detect diseases from medical scans faster and more accurately than traditional methods. How agricultural systems use ML to predict crop yields and detect plant diseases from drone images. How traffic systems in smart cities use ML to reduce congestion in real time.

Each example made the field feel more urgent and more exciting than the last.

### What This Did to My Motivation

Before that class I studied ML because it was part of our curriculum. After it I studied because I genuinely wanted to understand the systems shaping the world around me. The motivation was no longer external — it became personal.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad understood that students do not just need to learn concepts. They need a reason to care about them. That lecture gave me mine.

### Where I Want to Go From Here

I do not know exactly which area of ML I will specialize in. But I know that this field touches every part of life that matters — health, education, environment, communication. And I want to be someone who contributes to it, not just someone who consumes it.

That ambition started in Dr. Bilal Ahmad's classroom.
