1. From Paper Only to a Smarter Mix
I used to believe that real studying only happened with a pen and a physical notebook. Screens were distractions, not tools — or so I thought. But in my second semester, a few friends introduced me to some simple digital tools and my entire approach to studying changed. I did not abandon my notebook — I still believe handwriting helps memory — but I started combining it with technology in a way that made revision faster, organisation easier, and learning more enjoyable. The key was learning which tools to use and for what purpose.
2. The Tools That Genuinely Helped Me
The first tool that changed my studying was YouTube. Not for entertainment — but for finding video explanations of concepts I could not understand from the textbook alone. Seeing a concept explained visually made it click in a way that reading alone never did. I also started using Google Docs to create shared notes with my study group, which saved us all time. For revision, I used a simple flashcard method on my phone to test myself before exams. These were not complicated tools — they were free, simple, and incredibly effective when used with focus and purpose.
3. The Right Balance Between Digital and Traditional
The most important lesson I learned about technology and studying is that it should support your focus, not replace it. Phones and laptops become distractions the moment you open the wrong app. I started using my phone with intention — I would set a specific purpose before picking it up. "I am opening YouTube to understand this one topic." "I am opening my notes to revise this chapter." That intention made all the difference. Technology is not the enemy of good studying. Distraction is. And distraction is a choice, not a device. ✨
