1. A Presence That Changed Everything
Every student has that one person — a teacher, a parent, or a mentor — who sees something in them before they see it in themselves. For me, that person was my teacher. She never let me settle for less than my best. When I made mistakes, she did not scold me — she explained, encouraged, and made me try again. She had a way of making every student feel like they were capable of greatness. I was lucky enough to have her at a time when I needed that belief the most.
2. The Words That Stayed With Me
I still remember one afternoon when I was feeling completely defeated after a difficult exam. She called me aside and said something I will never forget: "The fact that you are still here means you have not given up. That is already more than most people do." Those simple words changed something inside me that day. I stopped seeing my struggles as signs of failure and started seeing them as signs that I was still fighting. Her words became a voice in my head every time I wanted to quit.
3. What True Support Looks Like
Support does not always look like grand gestures. Sometimes it is a teacher who takes extra time to explain a concept. Sometimes it is a parent who wakes up early just to make sure you have eaten before your exam. Sometimes it is a friend who shares their notes without being asked. I have been blessed with all of these. And because of that support, I carry a responsibility — to pass that same belief forward. One day, I want to be that person for someone else. The one who believes in them before they believe in themselves. 💪
