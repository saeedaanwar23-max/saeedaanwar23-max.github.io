## The Subject Everyone Underestimates

When we started our first semester at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, many students thought Programming Fundamentals would be the easiest subject. "It is just basic coding," people said. "We will manage."

Those same students struggled the most by mid-semester.

I was almost one of them — until **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** helped me understand why this subject is the most important foundation of our entire degree.

## What Fundamentals Actually Mean

Dr. Bilal Ahmad said something in class that I have never forgotten:

> *"If your fundamentals are weak, every advanced topic you study will be built on sand. The building will look tall — but it will fall."*

Programming Fundamentals is not about memorizing syntax. It is about learning **how to think**. How to break a big problem into smaller steps. How to write logic before writing code. How to debug with patience instead of panic.

## The First Time I Truly Understood

I remember struggling with loops for two full weeks. I could read the code but I could not write it myself. One day, Dr. Bilal Ahmad asked me to explain the logic in plain Urdu before writing a single line of code.

That changed everything. When I said it out loud — *"repeat this step until the condition is false"* — the code suddenly wrote itself.

## Fundamentals Are the Bridge to Machine Learning

Dr. Bilal Ahmad also connected this directly to Machine Learning. He explained that ML models are built on loops, conditions, functions, and data structures — the exact things we were learning. Without strong fundamentals, we cannot understand how algorithms work.

This connection gave me a reason to take every lab seriously, even when the tasks felt too simple.

## My Advice to Fellow Students

Do not rush the basics. Every concept you skip today will slow you down tomorrow. Spend extra time on loops, functions, and arrays. They will appear in every subject you study after this.
