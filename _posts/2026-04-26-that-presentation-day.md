That Presentation Day — Facing Fear With Fear
Stage Fright
The most terrifying day of my life was when I had to present in front of the class for the very first time. My hands were shaking, my voice was shaking, and I had forgotten my script. For a moment everything froze — just me and my anxiety. Those 5 minutes felt like the longest 5 minutes of my life.
What Happened After
The presentation ended. My voice had trembled, but I did not stop. The class clapped — maybe out of sympathy, but they clapped. The teacher said: "You finished — that was the most important thing." That day I understood that perfection is not the goal — completion is. Every time you step onto a stage it gets a little easier.
Public Speaking Is a Skill, not a Gift
Many people think public speaking comes naturally to some people. It does not. Everyone learned it. Everyone is nervous. The only difference is that those who practice are nervous less often. I started practicing after that day — at home, in front of a mirror, in front of friends. Each time a little better. The fear did not disappear but I gained control over it. ✨
