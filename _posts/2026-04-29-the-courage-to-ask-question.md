## The Courage to Ask Questions — Raising Your Hand in Class
### Why It Feels Scary
I used to never raise my hand in class. The fear was real — what if people laugh? What if the teacher thinks I do not know anything? This is a fear many students share silently. But because of this fear I used to go home carrying half-understood concepts. And trying to understand them alone at home took twice as long.
### The First Time I Asked
One day I genuinely did not understand a concept and I raised my hand. The teacher explained it in detail. Then they said: "That was a really good question — a lot of people have the same confusion." That moment changed my world. My question was not just mine — it belonged to many people. And by asking, I helped everyone.
### Questions Are the Path to Progress
The student who never asks only learns what the teacher taught. The student who asks deepens their own understanding. The world's greatest scientists, engineers, philosophers — they all kept asking questions. Asking questions is not weakness — it is intellectual courage. Be brave. Raise your hand. Ask. ✨
