## Sleep Before Exams — Why Staying Up All Night Is Wrong
### The All-Nighter Myth
We have all heard that top students study through the night. But this is a dangerous myth. I too stayed up until 3am many times before exams — and walked into the exam hall the next morning with a blank mind. I had studied a lot, but nothing was coming back. The reason? Sleep deprivation literally blocks memory.
### What Science Says
Research shows that during sleep, the brain processes and stores everything it learned during the day. This means if you sleep 6 or more hours, your brain does its own homework before the exam — without you having to do anything extra. If you studied but did not sleep — that studying may be wasted. Sleep is part of preparation, not laziness.
### What I Do Now
Today I go to sleep early the night before an exam — between 10 and 11pm. I set my alarms, put the phone away, and let go of the feeling that I should study just a little more. That one change genuinely improved my exam performance. Sleep well, wake up, and everything will be there. 😴📚
