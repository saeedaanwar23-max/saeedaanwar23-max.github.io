## The Day the Project Was Announced

When **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** announced that we would be working on a Machine Learning project, the whole class went quiet. Some students smiled nervously. Others started whispering, "What even is an ML project?"

I was somewhere in between — excited but completely lost.

## Starting From Zero

I opened my laptop that evening and stared at a blank screen for a long time. I knew what ML meant in theory — thanks to Dr. Bilal Ahmad's earlier explanation — but turning that knowledge into actual code felt like climbing a mountain with no path.

The first challenge was simple: **what problem do we solve?**

Dr. Bilal Ahmad had told us: *"Start with a problem you actually care about. Data is everywhere. The question is — what question do you want to answer?"*

## Choosing a Problem

After discussions with my group, we decided to work on something relatable to students — predicting whether a student might struggle in their semester based on study hours, attendance, and sleep patterns.

It was small. It was basic. But it was **ours**.

## What the Process Taught Me

Building the project taught me more than any lecture could. I learned:

- **Data is messy.** Real data is never clean. We spent more time cleaning our dataset than training our model.
- **Simple models can be powerful.** Our first working model used basic linear regression — nothing complex — but it gave us real predictions.
- **Errors are not failures.** Every error message was a teacher. Dr. Bilal Ahmad had said this before, and I finally understood it in practice.

## The Moment It Worked

The moment our model gave its first correct prediction, I genuinely felt something shift inside me. This was not just code. This was a machine that had **learned** from examples.

That feeling — I want to chase it for the rest of my career.

## Thank You, Dr. Bilal Ahmad

None of this would have started without the guidance and encouragement of Dr. Bilal Ahmad. He gave us the courage to try, even when we did not feel ready.
