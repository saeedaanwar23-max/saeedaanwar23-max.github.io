1. The Day Everything Felt Too Heavy
There was a point in my first semester when I genuinely considered stopping. Not for a dramatic reason — just because the weight of everything had built up quietly over weeks. The syllabus felt endless, my energy was low, and I felt like no matter how hard I tried, I was always falling behind. I remember sitting at my desk one evening staring at my open notebook and feeling completely empty. The words on the page meant nothing. The goals I had set felt unreachable. In that moment, quitting felt like the only way to breathe again.
2. What Pulled Me Back From the Edge
I did not have a single powerful moment that fixed everything. It was smaller than that. My mother brought me a cup of tea without saying anything. She just sat with me quietly. That small act of love reminded me that I was not alone in this. The next day, I talked to one of my close friends and she said: "You are not behind. You are just tired. Those are different things." That distinction stayed with me. I was not failing — I was exhausted. And exhaustion can be fixed with rest, not by quitting.
3. What I Know Now That I Did Not Know Then
Looking back, I understand now that every serious student hits a wall at some point. It is not a sign of weakness — it is a sign that you have been pushing yourself hard. The students who succeed are not the ones who never struggle. They are the ones who choose to keep going even on the days they do not feel like it. My lowest point did not break me — it built me. It taught me that I was stronger than I thought, and that asking for help is not giving up. It is one of the bravest things a student can do. ✨
