## The Poison of Comparison — Measure Yourself Against Yourself
### Comparing Yourself to Classmates
Social media has created a real problem: we compare our behind-the-scenes to everyone else's highlight reel. She seems sharper in class, she seems more confident, her marks are higher — thinking all of this slowly destroys your own confidence. I did this too. Far too much. And it made me feel very small.
### Building Your Own Baseline
One day I thought: what if I only compared myself to myself? To last month, to last semester? I started a growth diary where I wrote: "Today I did something I could not do yesterday." That was a game changer. I was growing every day — but comparing myself to others had made that growth invisible.
### Your Journey Is Your Own
The person who seems ahead today has their own struggles you know nothing about. And the person who seems behind you today may overtake you tomorrow. Every journey is different. Find your own rate of growth, recognise your own strengths, and keep your own direction clear. Comparison is a thief — it steals your time, your confidence, and your joy. Compete with yourself, not with others. 💪
