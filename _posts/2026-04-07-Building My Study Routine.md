1. The Routine I Thought Would Work
At the start of my first semester, I made a very ambitious study timetable. I wrote it neatly in my notebook, colour-coded every subject, and even added break times. It looked perfect on paper. But within a week, that beautiful timetable was completely ignored. The problem was not the plan — it was that the plan did not match real life. Some days lectures ran late. Some days I was too tired. Some days unexpected tasks came up. I quickly learned that a rigid timetable is not always your friend when life refuses to follow a schedule.
2. Finding What Actually Worked
After failing at my perfect timetable, I shifted to a simpler approach. Instead of planning every hour, I started setting daily goals. Each morning I would write down three things I must finish today. Just three. That small change made a huge difference. It reduced my overwhelm and gave me a clear direction every day. I also discovered that studying in the early morning worked much better for me than studying at night. My mind was fresher, the house was quieter, and I could focus without distractions. Small adjustments like these built my real routine over time.
3. The Lesson About Consistency Over Perfection
The biggest thing I learned from building my study routine is that consistency matters more than perfection. It is better to study for 30 focused minutes every day than to study for 5 hours once a week. Missing one day does not ruin your routine — giving up after missing one day does. I stopped punishing myself for imperfect days and started celebrating the days I showed up even when I did not feel like it. That mindset shift turned my routine from something I struggled with into something I was actually proud of. 📚
