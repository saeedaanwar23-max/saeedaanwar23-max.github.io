How to Take Notes That Actually Work
My First Mistake
In the beginning I used to write the entire textbook into my notes. Word for word. Page after page. Then before exams I would look at my notes and panic — there was too much. Taking notes is a skill that has to be learned, and that skill is: filtering. Not writing everything — writing what matters.
How the Cornell Method Changed Everything
One day I watched a video about the Cornell Notes method. Divide the page into three parts: cues, notes, summary. After the lecture write questions in the side column. At the bottom of the page summarize everything in one paragraph. This method makes studying active — you are not just writing, you are thinking. This one method cut my revision time to a quarter of what it used to be.
Revisiting Notes Is Essential
Read your notes again within 24 hours of writing them — research says reviewing within this window doubles retention. I built a habit: just 10 minutes every night to glance over that day's notes. This small habit saved me enormously at exam time. Writing good notes is half the work — revisiting them is the other half. ✨
