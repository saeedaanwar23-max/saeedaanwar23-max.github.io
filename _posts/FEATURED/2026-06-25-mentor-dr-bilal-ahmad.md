### Portfolio Purpose
## Early Confusion About Our Degree

During the early weeks of university life at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, one of the most common concerns among students in our class was about our degree program. We were enrolled in BSc Computer Engineering under SEC instead of PEC, and this created a lot of confusion and uncertainty. Many students, including myself, started questioning whether we were in the right field — worried about future jobs, degree value, and career options. This stress hit us right at the start of our university journey.

## How Dr. Bilal Ahmad Changed Everything

At that time, our Programming Fundamentals teacher, **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**, played a very important role in guiding us. He was not only knowledgeable but also someone who truly understood student mindset.

One day, he addressed this issue directly in class. Instead of ignoring our concerns, he gave us a clear and practical perspective that none of us had considered before.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained that in computer engineering and software-related fields, success does not depend on degree titles or registration bodies. It depends on **skills**.

> *"Your skills will define your future, not just your degree title."*

He encouraged us to focus on learning programming, building problem-solving abilities, and working on real projects. He shared examples of successful people in the tech industry who achieved great results through skills alone — not through formal titles.

## Personal Impact

For me personally, this was a turning point. I was already struggling with some concepts due to limited prior background. His guidance gave me the confidence to stop worrying about things outside my control and invest that energy into actual learning.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching style matched his beliefs. He encouraged logical thinking, deep understanding of concepts, and consistent coding practice rather than memorization. He was approachable, patient, and always ready to help.

## The Lesson That Stayed With Me

This experience taught me something I carry to this day — success in technology depends more on skills, consistency, and the right attitude than on labels and titles.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad was not just a teacher. He was a mentor who helped us move past confusion and pointed us toward the right path. His words continue to motivate me as I move forward in my journey as a Computer Engineering student.
