## The Word That Confused Me First

The first time I heard the word **Machine Learning**, I had no idea what it meant. I imagined machines sitting in a classroom, reading books. That sounds funny now — but that was honestly my first thought.

It was in our Programming Fundamentals class at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, when **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** first introduced this concept to us. And from that moment, something changed inside me.

## What Dr. Bilal Ahmad Explained

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained Machine Learning in the simplest possible way. He said:

> *"Machine Learning is not about teaching machines to think like humans. It is about giving machines enough examples so they can find patterns on their own."*

He gave us a very relatable example — spam emails. When your email app automatically sends junk mail to the spam folder, that is Machine Learning at work. No one manually told the app what spam looks like. It learned from thousands of examples.

## Why This Matters for Us as Computer Engineers

As Computer Engineering students, understanding Machine Learning is no longer optional. It is the foundation of modern software, healthcare systems, recommendation engines, and self-driving cars.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad told us that the earlier we start understanding the **basics** — data, patterns, and predictions — the stronger our foundation will be for everything that comes later.

## My First Step

After that class, I went home and searched "What is Machine Learning" for the first time. I watched one video. Then another. The curiosity had been sparked — and Dr. Bilal Ahmad was the one who lit that spark.
