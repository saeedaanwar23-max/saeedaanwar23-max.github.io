## The Word That Used to Intimidate Me

**Algorithm.** Before university, this word made me feel like I was not smart enough to study Computer Engineering. It sounded too technical, too mathematical, too advanced for someone like me.

Then I attended **Dr. Bilal Ahmad's** Programming Fundamentals class at UET Lahore — and that fear started to disappear.

## What an Algorithm Actually Is

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained algorithms in the most simple and memorable way:

> *"An algorithm is just a recipe. You have ingredients — that is your data. You have steps — that is your logic. And at the end, you get a result. That is all it is."*

I remember smiling at that explanation. A recipe. Of course. I had been following algorithms my whole life — I just did not know the word for it.

## Algorithms in Daily Life

Think about how you get ready for university in the morning:
1. Wake up when the alarm rings
2. If it is raining — take an umbrella
3. Pack your bag
4. Leave the house

That is an algorithm. A sequence of steps, with conditions, that leads to a result. Every computer program is a more complex version of this same idea.

## Algorithms in Machine Learning

In ML, algorithms are the tools that help a machine **find patterns** in data. Different algorithms work better for different problems:

- **Linear Regression** — predicting a number (like exam scores)
- **Decision Trees** — making yes/no decisions step by step
- **K-Nearest Neighbors** — comparing new data to the most similar old data

Dr. Bilal Ahmad taught us that choosing the right algorithm is a skill that develops with practice and understanding — not just memorization.

## The Confidence He Gave Us

What I appreciated most was that Dr. Bilal Ahmad never made us feel that algorithms were beyond our reach. He broke every concept into relatable parts. He used examples from real life, from our degree, and from industry.

He made us feel that we **belong** in this field.

## Start Simple, Then Go Deep

If you are just beginning, do not rush to the most complex algorithms. Master the basics first. Understand **why** an algorithm works before **how** it works. The depth will come naturally.
