## Making Mistakes Is Fine — Hiding Them Is Not
### Mistakes and Shame
I remember when I gave a wrong answer in class. The whole class heard it. My face turned red, my heart sank. In that moment it felt like the world had ended. But today when I think about it, I smile. That "mistake" — I still remember that concept more clearly than anything else from that class — precisely because I got it wrong.
### Good Students Make Mistakes
I have read that the brain learns more from mistakes — because when an error occurs, dopamine and attention both work together at the same time. This means mistakes are not just embarrassing — they are biologically the most effective moments for learning. The student who only gives safe answers does not learn nearly as much as the one who tries and sometimes fails.
### Being Honest with Yourself
If you make a mistake, own it. Tell your teacher, tell your classmate, tell yourself. Hiding it takes more energy and breaks trust. I built the habit of acknowledging my mistakes — it makes me feel lighter, more honest, and more trustworthy in the long run. Make mistakes. Have the courage to admit them. 💪
